01 · Section One

Company Overview

Four related companies, one ecosystem. You touch the books of two of them — and bill work to a third.

🏙️

Baum Realty Group (BRG)

Chicago's premier retail real estate firm since 1991, founded by David Baum (CEO). Brokerage: tenant rep, landlord rep, investment sales, national services. ~20+ contractor brokers. You keep BRG's corporate books — broker commissions are the heartbeat.

🏢

Baum Asset Management (BAM)

Third-party commercial property management, co-founded by David & Mike Demetriou (President) in 2017. 2.5MM+ sq ft, Chicagoland + select national markets. You keep BAM's corporate books and coordinate with property-level bookkeeping.

📍

Strategic Location Advisors

Mike's location-consulting venture (2023). Light accounting footprint — know it exists.

🛠️

Baum Revision

David's development company — separate from BRG and BAM, but you'll issue billback invoices to it (e.g., shared-services time). ⚠️ David's email is @baumrevision.com, not @baumrealty.com.

💡 The intercompany web is the job. Money moves constantly between BRG, BAM, Baum Revision, and properties — rent, insurance, payroll, bank fees, shared services. Getting entity and class coding right is most of the battle.

04 · Section Four

IT, Security & Remote Setup

One login. One portal. Real guardrails.

🔑

SSO (Microsoft Entra)

One password, everything. 2FA via Authenticator, pops every 5–6 days or on IP change. Entry points: myapps.microsoft.com and baum.tools — bookmark both.

🎫

ETS = IT

Always email ETS, never Jacob directly — no ticket, no coverage. Critical for remote workers.

🗄️

Box, not local

All files live in Box. The Collateral folder is the firm's filing cabinet — W-9s, lien waivers, templates, trackers.

🌐

Remote / VM

Remote staff connect via Microsoft VM through Entra SSO. Known VM latency/disconnect issues — ETS ticket if persistent. HubStaff runs on the VM (Maria coordinates).

🛡️

Security posture

Enterprise spam/anti-phishing; strict filters (may block legitimate international mail). Full audit trail on everything.

🔐

Credentials

System credentials (e.g., insurance portals) are documented in Notion — never in personal notes or email. Ask Mike where before creating any new account.

07 · Section Seven

CORE & the Deal-to-Payment Pipeline

Where brokerage money moves. Three rules are sacred.

📑 Deal ReportBroker submits Excel to Geri
›
🗃️ COREGeri creates deal + invoice
›
💵 Payment InClient funds received
›
➗ Split CalcCORE computes the split
›
🏦 bill.comPayable routed out
›
✅ PaidNext business day
🔒

Split confidentiality

Never share anything that lets someone calculate another broker's split. Restricted to Mike, Geri & the DB admin.

⚡

Next-business-day pay

We pay brokers the day after funds arrive. It's a recruiting differentiator — treat it as a hard SLA.

📈

Split thresholds

Most splits step up at a $200,000 revenue threshold (other tranches exist). CORE computes it — you verify against the approved split before paying.

⚠️ Common exceptions (all real): split % in CORE differs from what was approved · returned or duplicate deposits · deposits that don't match any invoice · outside-broker fees needing W-9/vendor setup first. When in doubt, hold the payment and ask Mike.

09 · Section Nine

Daily Reporting — SOD / EOD

Every working day, two short logs in the Notion EOD & SOD database. Non-negotiable.

🌅

Start of Day (SOD)

One to three bullets: today's priorities. Thirty seconds, first thing.

🌙

End of Day (EOD)

Bulleted list of what you actually did — specific enough that someone else could pick up the thread (names, entities, amounts, systems).

Why we're strict about this

  • It's how management stays out of your way — visible progress means no check-in meetings
  • It becomes SOPs, training material, and institutional memory (this very deck was built from EOD history)
  • It justifies property/entity billbacks for shared time
  • It feeds our AI tooling — searchable answers to "how did we handle X last time?"
✍️ Write EODs in plain, specific language. "Processed 2 commission deposits and related broker payments; flagged split discrepancy on X to Mike" beats "worked on commissions."

12 · Section Twelve

Document Hygiene

The mistake that's bitten us before.

📝

Track Changes

For any agreement or deal doc: Review ▸ Track Changes. Accept/reject deliberately — easy to approve an unintended date or number.

⚠️

Metadata

Word docs carry hidden authors, comments, and revision history. Before sending externally: File ▸ Info ▸ Check for Issues ▸ Inspect Document.
